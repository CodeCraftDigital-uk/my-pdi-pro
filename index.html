Automotive Compliance Tools for UK Used Car Dealers

Three professional digital compliance tools designed specifically for UK used car dealers, independent motor traders, online vehicle retailers, and motor trade compliance officers. All tools are aligned to current UK consumer legislation including the Consumer Rights Act 2015 and the Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013.

Used Vehicle PDI Report

A digital Pre-Delivery Inspection (PDI) report tool for UK used car dealers. Before handing over a used vehicle, dealers must document its mechanical condition to demonstrate due diligence under the Consumer Rights Act 2015. This tool records mechanical checks, tyre tread depths (in millimetres), brake performance, bodywork condition, interior condition, and electrics. The customer signs the report digitally at handover, and the completed PDI report can be exported as a PDF. A signed PDI report is critical evidence if a buyer later raises a fault or seeks to reject the vehicle under the CRA 2015 30-day right to reject or the 6-month burden of proof provisions.

Digital Distance Sale Pack

A Distance Sale Pack generator for UK used car dealers selling vehicles remotely — online, by phone, or without the buyer visiting the dealership premises before agreeing to purchase. Distance sales of vehicles are governed by the Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013 (CCR 2013). Dealers are legally required to provide mandatory pre-contract information and a written notice of the buyer's 14-day cooling-off period (right to cancel). Failure to do so is a criminal offence, and automatically extends the buyer's cancellation rights to 12 months. This tool generates a compliant distance sale information pack covering all mandatory CCR 2013 disclosures: trader identity, vehicle description, total price, delivery arrangements, cancellation rights, and refund policy.

UK Consumer Law Frameworks for Used Car Sales

The AutoProv Compliance Tools are built around the key pieces of UK legislation that govern the sale of used vehicles to consumers by motor traders.

Consumer Rights Act 2015 (CRA 2015)

The Consumer Rights Act 2015 is the primary legislation governing the sale of goods — including used vehicles — from a trader to a consumer in the United Kingdom. Under the CRA 2015, vehicles must be of satisfactory quality, fit for purpose, and as described. Key provisions for car dealers include:

  • 30-day short-term right to reject: Within 30 days of purchase, a consumer can reject the vehicle and receive a full refund if it is not of satisfactory quality.
  • 6-month burden of proof: If a fault appears within the first 6 months, it is presumed to have existed at the point of sale. The dealer must prove otherwise.
  • One opportunity to repair or replace: Between 30 days and 6 months, the dealer must be given one chance to repair or replace before the consumer can claim a refund.
  • Satisfactory quality standard: Accounts for age, mileage, and price paid — an older, higher-mileage car is held to a lower standard than a nearly-new vehicle.

Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013 (CCR 2013)

The Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013 govern distance selling and off-premises contracts in the UK, including the sale of vehicles online, by phone, or by any method where the buyer does not visit the dealer's premises before agreeing to purchase. Key requirements for car dealers under CCR 2013:

  • Mandatory pre-contract information: Traders must provide specific information about the goods, price, delivery, and cancellation rights before the contract is agreed.
  • 14-day cooling-off period: Consumers have 14 calendar days from delivery to cancel the contract without giving a reason.
  • Criminal offence: Failure to provide the required cancellation notice is a criminal offence under the CCR 2013.
  • Extended cancellation rights: If the cancellation notice is not provided, the consumer's cancellation rights extend to 12 months from the end of the original 14-day period.

Road Traffic Act

The Road Traffic Act imposes roadworthiness obligations on vehicle sellers. A used vehicle supplied by a dealer must be roadworthy at the point of sale. Selling an unroadworthy vehicle is an offence and also undermines the satisfactory quality standard under the Consumer Rights Act 2015. The PDI report helps evidence that a roadworthiness check was carried out before handover.

Sale of Goods Act 1979

The Sale of Goods Act 1979 (SGA 1979) established the foundational implied terms in sale of goods contracts, including that goods must be of merchantable (now satisfactory) quality and fit for purpose. While largely superseded for consumer sales by the Consumer Rights Act 2015, the SGA 1979 may still be relevant for business-to-business vehicle sales in the motor trade.

What is a PDI report and why do car dealers need one?

A PDI (Pre-Delivery Inspection) report is a formal checklist documenting a used vehicle's mechanical and cosmetic condition before it is handed to the buyer. It records checks on the engine, gearbox, brakes, tyres (including measured tread depths), bodywork, electrics, and interior. The customer signs the report at handover, confirming the vehicle's condition was disclosed. A completed PDI report is important evidence under the Consumer Rights Act 2015 — if a buyer later claims a fault existed at the point of sale, the PDI report documents the vehicle's condition and can support the dealer's position.

Do UK car dealers need a Distance Sale Pack?

Yes. If a UK car dealer sells a vehicle without the buyer visiting the dealer's premises before agreeing to purchase — for example through an online listing, phone call, or digital channel — the sale is classified as a distance sale under the Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013 (CCR 2013). The dealer must provide a written Distance Sale Pack containing mandatory pre-contract information and a formal notice of the buyer's 14-day cooling-off right to cancel. Not providing this documentation is a criminal offence and automatically extends the buyer's cancellation rights from 14 days to 12 months.

What consumer law applies to used car sales in the UK?
